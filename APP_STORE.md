## 3. Description

Gather turns days of back-and-forth over text into a few taps. Connect your calendar, make plans, and spend more time with the people you love.

HOW IT WORKS

Connect your Apple, Google, or Outlook calendar and Gather syncs your availability in the background. When you want to make a plan, pick your friends, choose a date or range of dates, and Gather shows every time slot where people are free. Pick a time, add a title and location, and the event is created with invitations sent automatically.

AVAILABILITY

Gather compares everyone's calendars and displays mutual free time in a visual layout. You can see at a glance whether everyone is free, who has a conflict, and which times are wide open. Filter by morning, afternoon, or evening. Set a duration. Exclude early mornings or late nights. Everything updates in real time.

PRIVACY

Friends only see when you're free or busy — never your event names, descriptions, or any other details. Gather stores availability only. Your calendar stays yours.

BLOCKED WINDOWS

Have times that are always off-limits? Block them — work hours, Sunday mornings, whatever — and Gather will mark you as busy during those windows even if your calendar is clear.

GROUPS

Organize friends into groups (roommates, coworkers, your usual crew) and select everyone with a single tap when making plans.

EVENTS

Your home screen shows upcoming events organized by Today, Tomorrow, This Week, and Later. Tap any event to see details, RSVP with one tap (Yes, No, or Maybe), or edit the title, time, location, and notes. Hosts can update anything; invitees can respond instantly.

LOCATIONS

Search for places and attach a location to any event. A map preview appears on the event detail page so everyone knows where to go.

NOTIFICATIONS

Get notified when someone invites you to an event, when an event is updated, or when you receive a friend request. You choose which notifications you want.

ADDING FRIENDS

Every account has a unique invite code. Share it to connect with friends on Gather, and accept or decline requests from within the app.
