Privacy & your data

Calendars stay private, by design

Gather accounts are created through Apple Sign In. Calendar integrations are entirely optional and exist only to find when you and your friends are free.

For all three calendar providers, Gather requests the minimum read-only access needed to show your availability. We read only free/busy time intervals — never event titles, descriptions, locations, or attendees.

Google Calendar

Scopes requested: calendar.calendarlist.readonly calendar.freebusy

  • Calendar names — so you can choose which calendars to include.
  • Free/busy time intervals — to compute your availability. No event titles or details.

We do not access Gmail, Google Contacts, Google Drive, or any other Google service. We do not sell or share your Google data. You can disconnect at any time from app settings.

Our use of Google data adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Outlook Calendar

Scopes requested: Calendars.Read offline_access

  • Microsoft account name & email — to identify which account is connected.
  • Calendar names — so you can choose which calendars to include.
  • Event times & free/busy status — to compute your availability. No event titles or details.

We do not access Outlook Mail, OneDrive, Contacts, or any other Microsoft service. We do not sell or share your Microsoft data. You can disconnect at any time from app settings.

Apple Calendar

Access method: native iOS calendar permission (NSCalendarsUsageDescription) — no OAuth or account connection required.

  • Calendar names — so you can choose which calendars to include.
  • Event times & free/busy status — to compute your availability. No event titles or details.
  • Write access (optional) — only used if you choose to save Gather events to your calendar.

You can revoke Apple Calendar access at any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Calendars.

For complete details on how we handle all calendar data, read our Privacy Policy.
